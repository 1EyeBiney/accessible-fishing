Architectural traps to flag
These need explicit decisions before implementation, or they will re-emerge as bugs:
**T-1. lastTarget coordinate frame (D-073 × drift).**
lastTarget.offset (c:\nbs\afish\src\casting\castPipeline.js:855)
is a POI-frame
{dx,dy} (c:\nbs\afish\src\casting\castPipeline.js:645)
measured from the boat at original lock time. If the boat drifts between cast N and cast N+1, the same offset points to a different world tile. Two valid resolutions: (a)
lastTarget (c:\nbs\afish\src\casting\castPipeline.js:1020)
stores POI-absolute coords (re-derive offset from current boat position on re-arm), or (b) declare lastTarget invalidates on any drift step ≥ threshold. Option (a) is closer to the camping loop's intent ("keep fishing the same brush pile"). Must be locked in a D-rule before Phase 2.
**T-2. Drift owner during FIGHT mode.** D-034 `fightLoop.js` ticks every 60ms; D-074 drift logic lives in `navigation.js`. While the player is fighting a fish, does the boat drift? Realism says yes; the player is occupied with rod tension and cannot oar. Architecturally clean: `fightLoop` does NOT touch position; `navigation.driftStep()` continues to run on its own schedule (likely
clock.every (c:\nbs\afish\src\core\clock.js:257)
at coarser granularity). The two are decoupled via state, not direct calls. But the input-conflict surface needs a rule: Numpad must be inert during FIGHT, just as during the cast FSM (mirror D-043's scan/cast mutex).
**T-3. D-040 Repositioning Penalty interaction.** Between-casts drift can push the boat to the POI boundary. The 5-minute snap penalty (D-040) was designed when drift was only triggered by D-002 numpad inputs. Now it can fire passively. Either accept that as the cost of inattention (good!) or add a TTS warning before the snap fires. **Recommend: keep the penalty mechanically, but add an "approaching boundary" announcement at e.g. 80% radius** — a navigation-side concern, not a brief-level decision.
**T-4. Cast anchor (H-019) — no change needed, but worth reasserting.** Each new TARGET_LOCKED re-anchors. The drift between casts does NOT retroactively move past casts' anchors. H-019 already covers this correctly.

That is fine for development — drift simply isn't required for the fight to be testable — but the *gameplay loop* feels broken until Phase 2 lands. **Flag for Phase 1 test plan only**, not a brief issue.
