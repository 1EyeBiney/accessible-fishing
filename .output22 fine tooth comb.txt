Vulnerability #1 — activeTackle Mutation Boundary is UNDEFINED (your v1.12 note was correct)
The flaw: D-067 says castPipeline and equipment.js "ONLY have access to items in the activeTackle partition" during TOURNAMENT_ACTIVE. But equipment.js Public API in §9 declares damageItem(id, kind) and vigor(baitId) — and both must mutate during a tournament (D-051 live-bait vigor, D-044 rod/lure durability). D-067 conflates two different mutation classes:
**Set-membership mutation** (adding/removing items) — MUST be locked during TOURNAMENT_ACTIVE
**Per-item state mutation** (durability tick-down, vigor decay, "this item is broken") — MUST remain enabled during TOURNAMENT_ACTIVE
A coding agent reading D-067 literally will either (a) reject all mutation and silently break durability, or (b) miss the lock entirely and allow mid-tournament tackle swaps. Either failure is silent and replay-corrupting.
Patch: New hazard H-017 formalizes the boundary.
Vulnerability #2 — Same-Tick Bot Catch Race vs H-016
The flaw: H-016 says SIMULATED_CATCH must carry pre-computed leaderboardImpact (computed → committed → emitted in a single atomic block per catch). This works for one bot. It fails when two bots' clock.every(...) cooldowns coincide on the same atMs (which is statistically certain across a long tournament given D-060's discrete cooldown buckets):
Bot A's tick fires → computes impact against current leaderboard (rank 4→2)
Bot B's tick fires (same `atMs`) → computes impact against **same pre-A leaderboard** (rank 6→3)
A commits + emits → leaderboard now reflects A
B commits + emits → but B's payload says "newRank: 3" computed against stale state; actual post-A-and-B rank may be 4
TTS announces wrong standings. Replay determinism survives (math is deterministic) but the announcement content is wrong. H-016 covers ordering against the bus, not ordering across simultaneous schedulers.
Patch: New hazard H-018 — aiBots.js MUST serialize same-tick bot processing: each bot's compute→commit→emit completes before the next bot begins.
Vulnerability #3 — Loadout Rigging has NO MODE in the FSM
The flaw: D-067 says Loadout Rigging happens in hubMenu.js "before entering a tournament." But:
§9 declares `hub/*` is **HUB-mode only** and **unmounts on enter-TOURNAMENT_ACTIVE** (D-025).
D-017 lists modes: HUB, **TOURNAMENT_BRIEFING**, TOURNAMENT_ACTIVE, TOURNAMENT_RESULTS.
TOURNAMENT_BRIEFING currently has **no declared owner or behavior** in the brief.
Where does `boats.maxRods/maxLures` validation run? Where is the rigging FSM mounted? The brief doesn't say.
A coding agent will either (a) cram the rigging FSM into hubMenu.js and trip D-025 when the player commits, or (b) silently skip validation, allowing tournaments to start with invalid (or empty) activeTackle.
Patch: New decision D-069 anchors Loadout Rigging to TOURNAMENT_BRIEFING mode, owned by tournament.js, blocking transitionTo(TOURNAMENT_ACTIVE) until validation passes.
