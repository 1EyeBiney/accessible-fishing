Architectural Review — Phase 1 Refinements (v1.17 candidate)
1. Analysis
Refinement A — State Announcer Layer
Verdict: necessary, not optional. AFish's audio surface today carries continuous (tension), edge (phase change), categorical (TTS), and procedural (ping) channels — but no narrative channel. A screen-reader user transitioning from LURE_SELECT → ARMED → CAST_LANDED → FISH_HOOKED currently hears: lure name, metronome ticks, pitch sweep, splash, then silence until the first nibble. Three of those have no semantic label. "Time to Cast / Fish / Fight" is the missing reading-comprehension layer.
Two architectural cautions:
**Source of truth.** Announcer cues must NOT be re-derived in `audio/ttsQueue.js` from low-level events (that creates duplicate state machines). They are best emitted as first-class **`STATE_ANNOUNCE { token }`** events by the owner of each transition: `castPipeline` for ARMED + CAST_LANDED, `fightLoop` for FISH_HOOKED.
**TTS priority.** These cues are **NORMAL** priority (D-062 ladder). They must NOT be HIGH/URGENT — those are reserved for catch-impact events. They MUST coalesce: a re-cast within 3 seconds should not bark "Time to Cast" twice in a row, and the cue should be silenced if a NIBBLE_THUD is queued within the next ~750 ms (this is the basis for H-022 below).
Refinement B — The Retrieval Pivot
Verdict: strong design. The current FSM has a brutal honesty problem: a bad cast (silent splash, no bite within ~30 s) leaves the player passively waiting for the clock to age them out. Two retrieve options turn idle dread into a tactical decision.
Looking at the numbers against the clock:
Option Clock cost Spook cost When to use
Wait it out ~30–90 s (bite timer) 0 First cast, fresh water
Soft Retrieve 5 s 0 Bad cast on undisturbed cover
Power Rip 2 s +1 (decays 12 s per D-038) Bad cast where pressure already matters more than spook
The choice is real: in early game (cold water, fresh POI) Soft is correct; late tournament (clock pressure, water already pressured) Rip is correct. The 12-second spook decay (D-038) means Rip is locally recoverable but expensive if chained.
One refinement I'd push back on slightly: **Power Rip should drop the lure at the boat, NOT clear
lastTarget (c:\nbs\afish\src\casting\castPipeline.js:1020)
.** This preserves D-073 camping — the player ripped the cast because they spooked themselves, not because they want a new target. The next SPACEBAR-in-IDLE should still camp the retained target. If you'd prefer Rip to also clear lastTarget as a heavier "reset" action, the brief amendment below can be adjusted.
A non-obvious trap: both retrieves must cancel any in-flight bite timer scheduled by D-032's strike pipeline (a bite may already be scheduled at CAST_LANDED + N ms before the player rips). Without that cancel, a ghost BITE_THUD could fire after the FSM has returned to IDLE.
Refinement C — Reeling Control Reversal + Tactical Pump (D-031 amendment)
Verdict: ergonomically correct, mechanically sound. Pulling DOWN on a rod is reeling in fishing reality; pushing UP is giving drag. Players already learned ARROW_UP for Tap 1/3 backswing and ARROW_DOWN for Tap 5 splashdown — extending ARROW_DOWN as "the active aggression key" is consistent. The current scheme (Spacebar = reel, ARROW_DOWN = drag) is the inverse of intuition and should never have shipped.
Tactical Pump analysis:
Cost Benefit Balance
1-second clock penalty per tap Stamina drain on fish (shorter fight) Self-limiting — spammers lose more clock than they save
Modest line-wear (durability) Same Equipment cost forces rotation
Replaces D-031 SPACEBAR's old role Frees Spacebar for an optional mechanic, not a mandatory one Accessibility win — non-pumpers can still land fish
The math needs one specific guard: the stamina drain per pump must be less than the natural stamina drain from sustained reel-in (ARROW_DOWN held). Otherwise pump-spam becomes the dominant strategy and ARROW_DOWN becomes vestigial. Recommend PUMP_STAMINA_DRAIN = 0.5 × REEL_STAMINA_DRAIN_PER_TICK so a pump is worth roughly half a second of held reel, but costs a full second of clock — net negative for spam, net positive for tactical use on a tired fish.
One conflict to flag: D-031 currently declares a STRICT MUTEX between SPACEBAR (reel) and ARROW_DOWN (drag). Under the new scheme, SPACEBAR is no longer reel — it is the Pump. There is no longer a natural mutex pair, because Pump is a momentary edge event and reel/drag are continuous holds. The mutex rule dissolves, not transfers. Holding ARROW_UP + ARROW_DOWN simultaneously is the new mutex case (reel + drag cancel each other → idle decay).
Refinement D (implied) — H-022 Announcement Overlap
Verdict: must exist. The acoustic stack at the bite-to-fight transition is:
```
T+0.000s   Splash SFX (procedural)
T+0.050s   "Time to Fish" TTS (NORMAL)
T+30-90s   Procedural nibble pings (1..N)
T+~T_thud  BITE_THUD edge cue (procedural)
T+~T_thud  HOOKSET_WINDOW opens (300-750 ms, D-033/D-050)
```
If "Time to Fish" arrives at T+0.050 and a fast bite timer fires at T+0.800, a slow TTS voice (some screen readers run ~120 wpm = ~500 ms/word, ~2 s for "Time to Fish") will still be speaking when the nibble ping arrives. The player misses the nibble. This is a fatal accessibility regression.
Mitigation must live at the ttsQueue level: state-announcer cues are preemptable by procedural fight/bite events and deferred if a nibble is already queued.

2c. New hazard to append to §8 (after H-021)
```
- H-022 Announcement Overlap (LOCKED v1.17): The new `STATE_ANNOUNCE` channel (D-080) speaks short narrative phrases ("Time to Fish") at NORMAL priority. A TTS phrase of even 1.5–2 s can mask a procedural BITE_NIBBLE ping or BITE_THUD edge cue if those arrive before the phrase finishes. Masking a nibble = silent loss of the hookset window (D-033) = silent loss of the fish to a screen-reader user. Mitigation, owned by `audio/ttsQueue.js`: (a) any STATE_ANNOUNCE token is **preemptable** — when a `BITE_NIBBLE`, `BITE_THUD`, `FIGHT_THRESHOLD_CROSSED`, or `FIGHT_RESOLVED` arrives mid-utterance, the queue invokes the platform TTS cancel/stop method immediately and re-arms; (b) STATE_ANNOUNCE is **deferred** if a bite timer's scheduled fire time falls within `(now, now + estimatedUtteranceMs + 250ms)` — the announcer waits and is requeued after the bite resolves or the window passes; (c) two STATE_ANNOUNCE tokens within 3 s coalesce per D-080. URGENT and HIGH priority cues (D-062 catch announcements) preempt STATE_ANNOUNCE under the existing ladder — no new code path needed for those. The procedural finder ping (D-065) and procedural metronome/sweep (D-021 addendum) are not TTS and are unaffected by this hazard; they share an audio bus but not the TTS queue.
```
