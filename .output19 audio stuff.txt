Procedural Audio Math — Design
1. Tension-to-Hz Map (Fight Loop)
Goal: continuous pitch readout that's informative, not fatiguing, and works for screen-reader users who will be listening to this for 30–60 seconds per fight.
Range Selection
Parameter Value Reasoning
TENSION_HZ_BASE 220 Hz Concert A3 — comfortable, low-fatigue baseline; below telephony band so doesn't clash with TTS (300–3400 Hz)
TENSION_HZ_MAX 880 Hz Concert A5 — exactly 2 octaves above base; still well below the 2–4kHz ear-fatigue band
Octave span 2 Wide enough to feel dramatic, narrow enough to avoid piercing peak
Ramp Curve — EXPONENTIAL (musical perception)
Linear Hz is psychoacoustically wrong — human pitch perception is logarithmic. A linear ramp from 220→880 would feel "rushed at the top." Exponential gives even perceptual steps per Δtension.
$$
\text{freqHz}(t) = \text{BASE} \cdot 2^{(t \cdot \text{OCTAVES})} = 220 \cdot 2^{2t}
$$
t = 0.00 → 220 Hz (calm)
t = 0.25 → 311 Hz
t = 0.50 → 440 Hz (concert A4 — exactly middle)
t = 0.75 → 622 Hz
t = 0.95 → 822 Hz (SNAP_DANGER threshold — audibly screaming)
t = 1.00 → 880 Hz (LINE_SNAPPED — pitch caps then terminal sting overrides)
Why NOT snap to a musical scale
Considered and rejected. Discrete stepping would hide small tension deltas that matter strategically (especially the 0.02 Δ-gate from D-035). Continuous ramp via Web Audio linearRampToValueAtTime(freqHz, atMs + rampToMs) preserves all the granularity the fight engine emits.
Anti-Fatigue Rules
Rule Mechanism
Volume duck at peak Above t ≥ 0.85, oscillator gain drops 30% — paradoxical, but stops the "screech" reflex while keeping pitch info
Sawtooth → sine blend Timbre crossfades from sine (calm, t=0) to soft-clipped sawtooth (urgent, t=1) — drama via harmonic richness, not loudness
Slack silence At t ≤ 0.05 (slack territory), oscillator mutes; replaced by a low "lapping water" noise bed so player isn't sat in dead silence during HOOK_SHAKEN grace
No ramp on edge events Phase / threshold events (D-035) use separate synth voices that don't compete with the tension oscillator
2. Target-Selector Audio Flow (Fish Finder Menu)
Problem: TTS reading "Candidate 1: 4 meters deep, gravel bottom, pressure level 2, scattered presence" every time the player presses ↓ is a brutal verbosity wall. Solution: sonify the numeric fields, speak only the categorical ones.
Two-Layer Cue per Menu Step
```
Player presses DOWN/UP arrow on menu
       │
       ├─→  PROCEDURAL "FINGERPRINT" PING  (≤ 300ms, plays first)
       │      └─ encodes: depth, pressure, presenceHint via pitch + noise + envelope
       │
       └─→  TTS SHORT-FORM SUMMARY  (queued NORMAL priority)
              └─ "Cover: timber edge"  (categorical-only, ≤ 1.2s)
```
The ping arrives in ~30ms — player can rapid-scan the menu by ear without waiting for TTS to finish each line. TTS catches up on the candidate they linger on.
Depth → Pitch (inverse: shallow = high)
$$
\text{pingHz}(\text{depthM}) = 440 \cdot 2^{-\text{depthM} / 6}
$$
Halves every 6 meters. Provides ~1 octave per ~6m of depth swing typical in v0.1 lakes.
depthM pingHz Mnemonic
0.5 467 Surface, bright
2.0 392 Shallow flat
6.0 220 Mid-column
10.0 156 Deep
15.0 110 Drop-off floor
Pressure → Noise Mix (D-039)
Pressure overlays a band-limited noise layer on the ping. Low pressure = pure tone; high pressure = "fizzy / hashy" tone. Player learns to associate pressure with "this candidate sounds dirty."
$$
\text{noiseGain}(p) = 0.05 + 0.15 \cdot \frac{p}{\text{MAX_PRESSURE}}
$$
pressure noiseGain Perceptual feel
0 0.05 Clean ping
2 0.11 Slight rasp
5 0.20 Audibly hashy
Presence Hint → Envelope (D-042, PRO+ finders only)
presenceHint Envelope
NONE Single 250ms ping
TRACE Single 250ms ping + faint 80ms tail
SCATTERED Two pings, 80ms apart
SCHOOLED Three pings, 60ms apart
Multi-ping envelope is instantly recognizable; no TTS needed for the most-checked field.
Spook → High-Shelf Filter Cut (PRO+ only)
High spook = high frequencies attenuated. The ping "sounds far away / muffled" when the tile is hot. Spook level 5 cuts everything above 800Hz by 12dB.
Boundary / Discipline
Rule Why
synthGraph.js exposes only playFinderPing({depthM, pressure, presenceHint, spook}) One entry point; consumers don't reach into Web Audio nodes
Ping is fire-and-forget (no queue, no coalescing) Menu navigation is player-paced — they can't outrun a 300ms ping by holding the arrow key (input adapter rate-limits)
